Table: grad_rates
Source: IPEDS Graduation Rate (GR) and Graduation Rate 200% (GR200) / Outcome components
IMPORTANT: This table has NOT been pre-filtered — it contains 2-year, 4-year, and less-than-2-year institutions. This app only covers 4-year institutions, so EVERY query must include the filter: WHERE ICLEVEL = 1

Two metric families live in this table (see the "Which metric" note below):
- GBA4/GBA5/GBA6 = bachelor's-degree completion within 4 / 5 / 6 years (the bachelor's-seeking cohort). GBA6RTT is THE default "graduation rate" for this app.
- GRRT = the overall graduation rate within 150% of normal time (the full Graduation Rate Survey cohort). Related to, but not identical to, GBA6.
All rate columns are whole-number percentages (integer, 0-100).

Columns:

Identifiers / metadata:
- iped_yr (text): IPEDS collection year label, formatted as a string like '2015-16', '2016-17', etc.
- year (integer): derived from iped_yr using the FIRST year in the label (e.g. "2015-16" → 2015). This represents the collection/reporting year, NOT the year students entered college — the underlying entering cohort started approximately 6 years earlier (e.g. year=2015 reflects outcomes for a cohort that entered around 2009). Use this column for filtering/sorting by year (e.g. WHERE year BETWEEN 2015 AND 2024).
- UNITID (integer): IPEDS unique institution identifier
- INSTNM (text): official institution name
- STABBR (text): 2-letter state abbreviation
- ICLEVEL (integer): institution level — 1 = four-or-more-year, 2 = at least 2 but less than 4 years, 3 = less than 2 years. ALWAYS filter to ICLEVEL = 1 for this app.
- CONTROL (integer): institution control — 1 = public, 2 = private not-for-profit, 3 = private for-profit

6-year bachelor's graduation rate (GBA6RT*) — THE primary/default metric for this app:
- GBA6RTT (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate as a whole-number percentage (e.g. 55 = 55%), total cohort
- GBA6RTM (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, men
- GBA6RTW (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, women
- GBA6RTWH (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, White, non-Hispanic
- GBA6RTBK (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, Black, non-Hispanic
- GBA6RTHS (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, Hispanic
- GBA6RTAS (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, Asian
- GBA6RTNH (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, Native Hawaiian/Other Pacific Islander
- GBA6RTAP (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, Asian/Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander COMBINED (legacy category — see the race note; do not mix with AS + NH in the same breakdown)
- GBA6RTAN (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, American Indian/Alaska Native
- GBA6RT2M (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, two or more races
- GBA6RTUN (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, race/ethnicity unknown
- GBA6RTNR (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, U.S. Nonresident

4- and 5-year bachelor's graduation rate (total cohort only — no subgroup breakdowns exist at 4 or 5 years):
- GBA4RTT (integer, 0-100): 4-year bachelor's graduation rate, total cohort
- GBA5RTT (integer, 0-100): 5-year bachelor's graduation rate, total cohort

Overall graduation rate within 150% of normal time (GRRT*, full GRS cohort). Distinct from the bachelor's-within-N-years GBA* metrics above:
- GRRTTOT (integer, 0-100): overall graduation rate within 150% of normal time, total cohort
- GRRTM (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, men
- GRRTW (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, women
- GRRTWH (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, White, non-Hispanic
- GRRTBK (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, Black, non-Hispanic
- GRRTHS (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, Hispanic
- GRRTAS (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, Asian
- GRRTNH (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, Native Hawaiian/Other Pacific Islander
- GRRTAP (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, Asian/Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander COMBINED (legacy category — see the race note; do not mix with AS + NH)
- GRRTAN (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, American Indian/Alaska Native
- GRRT2M (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, two or more races
- GRRTUN (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, race/ethnicity unknown
- GRRTNR (integer, 0-100): 150%-time graduation rate, U.S. Nonresident

Transfer-out rates:
- TRRTTOT (integer, 0-100): transfer-out rate, total GRS cohort
- GBATRRT (integer, 0-100): transfer-out rate, bachelor's cohort

Pell Grant / federal-loan recipient subgroups (data begins in collection year 2016; 2015 is NULL for these). Each group has a 6-year-bachelor's version (…BA6RT) and a 150%-time-overall version (…GRRTT):
- PGBA6RT (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, Pell Grant recipients
- SSBA6RT (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, subsidized-loan recipients who did NOT receive Pell
- NRBA6RT (integer, 0-100): 6-year bachelor's graduation rate, received neither Pell nor a subsidized loan
- PGGRRTT (integer, 0-100): 150%-time overall graduation rate, Pell Grant recipients
- SSGRRTT (integer, 0-100): 150%-time overall graduation rate, subsidized-loan recipients who did NOT receive Pell
- NRGRRTT (integer, 0-100): 150%-time overall graduation rate, received neither Pell nor a subsidized loan

Notes:
- Which metric to use: an unqualified "graduation rate" ALWAYS means the 6-year bachelor's rate (GBA6RT* family; GBA6RTT for the total). Use GBA4RTT / GBA5RTT only when the user explicitly says "4-year" / "5-year". Use the GRRT* family only when the user explicitly asks for the "overall," "150% of normal time," or "GRS" graduation rate. Never silently substitute one family for another.
- Rate columns are whole-number percentages (0-100), not decimals. Display them with a % sign, don't multiply by 100 again.
- Rate columns may be NULL where IPEDS suppressed small-n cells (or, for the Pell/loan columns, before 2016) — treat NULL as missing, never zero.
- Race categories: AP (GBA6RTAP / GRRTAP) is the legacy COMBINED "Asian/Native Hawaiian/Other Pacific Islander" category; AS (…AS, Asian) and NH (…NH, Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander) are the separate current categories. For a by-race breakdown prefer the split AS + NH; use AP only when the user explicitly wants the combined figure. Never include AP alongside AS and NH in the same breakdown — that double-counts.
- IMPORTANT — aggregate questions must be declined: this database contains institution-level rates only, with no cohort counts (numerators/denominators). Cross-institution aggregates (e.g. "the national graduation rate," "the average graduation rate of all 4-year institutions," "the overall rate in Massachusetts") cannot be computed correctly here: an unweighted average of institution rates differs substantially from the student-weighted aggregate that official sources like NCES publish (e.g. ~55% unweighted vs ~65% weighted), because institutions of very different sizes count equally. When a user asks for any cross-institution average, aggregate, or "overall/national/statewide rate," DECLINE to compute it and explain briefly: "This prototype holds institution-level rates without underlying cohort counts, so cross-institution averages would be unweighted and differ from official student-weighted figures (like NCES aggregates). I can compare specific institutions instead." Ranking and filtering across institutions (highest/lowest/top-N) remain fine — only averaging/aggregating across institutions is out of scope. (Note: the GRRT* "overall/150%-time" columns are a per-institution metric and are fine to query for a specific institution — this rule is only about averaging ACROSS institutions.)
- If a user asks about a specific year's graduation rate (e.g. "2020"), that reflects the collection/reporting year, not when students started college. If a user explicitly asks about "students who started in year X," compute the corresponding collection year as X + 6 rather than matching X directly against the year column.
- "Non-Pell" is ambiguous: the subsidized-loan (…SS…) and neither (…NR…) columns are separate groups. Default: combine them if a user asks for "non-Pell" without specifying further. Use the 6-year-bachelor's versions (SSBA6RT, NRBA6RT) by default; use the 150%-time versions (SSGRRTT, NRGRRTT) only if the user asked for the overall/150% rate.
- This table is NOT pre-filtered by institution level. Every query in this app must include WHERE ICLEVEL = 1 to restrict to 4-year institutions.
- Institution names can be ambiguous (e.g. over 20 institutions contain "Washington," including 3 separate University of Washington campuses). Use the confirm-before-execute flow for any non-exact or multi-match name lookup.

Named institution groups (not in the database — provided here since IPEDS has no group concept):
- Ivy League: Harvard University, Yale University, Princeton University, Columbia University in the City of New York, University of Pennsylvania, Brown University, Cornell University, Dartmouth College
- Users may also define their own ad hoc comparison sets by naming institutions directly; resolve these the same way as any individual institution lookup (exact match if unambiguous, confirm-before-execute if ambiguous).

Example question -> SQL pairs (tested against the live database):

1. Direct/unambiguous institution:
Q: "What's MIT's 6-year graduation rate in 2023?"
SQL: SELECT year, GBA6RTT FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM = 'Massachusetts Institute of Technology' AND year = 2023;

2. Ambiguous institution name (should trigger confirm-before-execute):
Q: "What's Cornell's graduation rate?"
Matches both 'Cornell University' and 'Cornell College' -> ask "Did you mean Cornell University or Cornell College?" before running any query.

3. Comparison across institutions:
Q: "Compare Harvard and Yale's graduation rates in 2023"
SQL: SELECT INSTNM, GBA6RTT FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM IN ('Harvard University','Yale University') AND year = 2023;

4. Pell subgroup:
Q: "What's the graduation rate gap between Pell and non-Pell students at Cornell University in 2023?"
SQL: SELECT year, PGBA6RT, SSBA6RT, NRBA6RT FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM = 'Cornell University' AND year = 2023;
(Remember: combine SSBA6RT and NRBA6RT as "non-Pell" unless the user specifies otherwise.)

5. Trend over time:
Q: "How has MIT's graduation rate changed over the last 10 years?"
SQL: SELECT year, GBA6RTT FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM = 'Massachusetts Institute of Technology' ORDER BY year;

6. Race/ethnicity subgroup:
Q: "Compare graduation rates by race at UC Berkeley in 2023"
SQL: SELECT year, GBA6RTWH, GBA6RTBK, GBA6RTHS, GBA6RTAS, GBA6RTAN, GBA6RT2M FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM = 'University of California-Berkeley' AND year = 2023;

7. Ranking/filter:
Q: "Which Massachusetts institution had the highest graduation rate in 2023?"
SQL: SELECT INSTNM, GBA6RTT FROM grad_rates WHERE STABBR = 'MA' AND year = 2023 AND GBA6RTT IS NOT NULL ORDER BY GBA6RTT DESC LIMIT 1;

8. Out-of-scope (should gracefully decline, not attempt SQL):
Q: "What's the average SAT score at Harvard?"
No SQL — respond: "I don't have admissions test score data — I can only answer questions about graduation and transfer-out rates for 4-year institutions."

9. Group-based trend visualization:
Q: "Show a 10-year trend line of Yale's graduation rate compared to other Ivy League schools"
SQL: SELECT INSTNM, year, GBA6RTT FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM IN ('Harvard University','Yale University','Princeton University','Columbia University in the City of New York','University of Pennsylvania','Brown University','Cornell University','Dartmouth College') ORDER BY INSTNM, year;
Render as a multi-line chart, one line per institution.

10. 4-year (or 5-year) rate — only when the user says so explicitly:
Q: "What's MIT's 4-year graduation rate in 2023?"
SQL: SELECT year, GBA4RTT FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM = 'Massachusetts Institute of Technology' AND year = 2023;

11. Compare completion horizons for one institution:
Q: "How do MIT's 4-, 5-, and 6-year graduation rates compare over time?"
SQL: SELECT year, GBA4RTT, GBA5RTT, GBA6RTT FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM = 'Massachusetts Institute of Technology' ORDER BY year;

12. Overall (150%-of-normal-time) rate — only when the user asks for the "overall / 150% / GRS" rate:
Q: "What's Cornell University's overall graduation rate within 150% of normal time in 2023?"
SQL: SELECT year, GRRTTOT FROM grad_rates WHERE INSTNM = 'Cornell University' AND year = 2023;
